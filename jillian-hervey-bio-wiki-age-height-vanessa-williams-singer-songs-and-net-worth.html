Jillian Hervey Biography

Jillian Hervey is an American singer, actress, dancer, and member of the group Lion Babe who was born and raised in Los Angeles, California, the United States. She is widely known for her songs including Jungle Lady(2016), Rockets(2017), and Hit the Ceiling(2017). As an actress, she is known for Grand Theft Auto V (2013), Girls5eva (2021), and TAKEMEANYWHERE (2018). She is the daughter of Ramon Hervey II and singer, actress, and former Miss America Vanessa Williams

Jillian Singer

Jillian and Lucas Goodman released a single entitled Treat Me Like Fire in December 2012. This was their first song as the duo Lion Babe, putting Jullian’s “towering cloud of golden curls and honeyed voice…in near-instant demand,” according to Vogue Magazine. The duo has subsequently collaborated with Childish Gambino, Pharrell Williams, and Mark Ronson.  They released another new single entitled Jungle Lady in 2014 with Essence saying “Talent must run in the family. Lion Babe played Lollapalooza in July 2015. Lion Babe released their first album, Begin in 2016

Jillian Actress

Jillian was cast in the Lifetime television movie The Courage to Love in 2010. The film was a historical drama starring her mother Vanessa Williams as Henriette Delille, a free woman of color who was born into a prominent New Orleans family in 1813. Reportedly Jillian was also offered a role on Gossip Girl when she was 19 but declined in favor of pursuing music. Jillian was cast in an episode of RuPaul’s Drag Race in 2012 performing the song “Glamazon” with contestants.

Jillian Beauty and Fashion

Jillian also became a representative for Pantene hair products in June 2016, the same year, she became a spokeswoman for MAC Cosmetics as part of a campaign known as Future Forward focused on up-and-coming musicians, including Dej Loaf and Tinashe, also women of color.
