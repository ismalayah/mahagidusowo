Skyla
フウロ Huuro

Skyla (Japanese: フウロ Huuro) is a Supporter card. It was first released as part of the Boundaries Crossed expansion.

Card text

Boundaries Crossed print
Search your deck for a Trainer card, reveal it, and put it into your hand. Shuffle your deck afterward.
You may play only 1 Supporter card during your turn (before your attack).
Rebel Clash print
Search your deck for a Trainer card, reveal it, and put it into your hand. Then, shuffle your deck.
You may play only 1 Supporter card during your turn.

Release information

This card was included as both a Regular and a Full Art card in the Boundaries Crossed expansion. The Regular print originates from the Japanese Cold Flare expansion and features artwork by Yusuke Ohmura, while the Full Art print originates from the Japanese Plasma Gale expansion and features artwork by Kanako Eo. The Regular print was later reprinted in the Japanese Black Kyurem-EX and White Kyurem-EX Battle Strength Decks, and Blastoise + Kyurem-EX Combo Deck. A special Mirror Holofoil version of the Regular print was made available for the Chespin Season of the 2013 Pokémon League Cycle.

The Regular print was reprinted again in the BREAKthrough expansion, previously released in the Japanese Raichu and Noivern BREAK Evolution Packs. A new Full Art print with artwork by Megumi Mizutani was released as part of the BREAKpoint expansion. In Japan, this print was a XY-P Promotional card given to customers in stores hosting Pokémon Card Gym meetings who purchased 2 boxes in any combination of Blue Shock or Red Flash upon their release. The Japanese print also features the Blue Shock and Red Flash expansion logos on the bottom of the card. The Regular print was reprinted in the Japanese Premium Trainer Box, utilizing its original card layout instead of the newly introduced layout for the Sun & Moon Era. The Best of XY subset, the Ultra Sun & Ultra Moon Premium Trainer Box, and the Ultra Sun Deck Build Box included further Regular reprints with this layout in Japan.

Skyla was reprinted during the Sword & Shield Series and was released as part of the Rebel Clash expansion with new artwork by Hideki Ishikawa, first released in the Japanese VMAX Rising subset. A new Full Art print with artwork by kirisAki was included in the Shining Fates expansion, first released in the Japanese Shiny Star V subset.

Origin
